# Diplomacia Position Papers Program
## Author & Reviewer Guidelines (v1.0)

## 1. About the Initiative

The Diplomacia Position Papers Archive is a structured, peer-reviewed platform for policy writing within the society.

Its objectives are to:
- Encourage structured policy thinking
- Promote evidence-based argumentation
- Train members in formal academic writing
- Create a permanent intellectual repository
- Foster a culture of constructive peer review

**This is not a blog. This is not opinion commentary. This is structured argumentative writing grounded in evidence and reasoning.**

## 2. Eligibility

- Open only to registered Diplomacia members
- Solo submissions only (unless otherwise announced)
- Maximum one submission per author per cycle
- Future expansion to alumni or external contributors may occur and will be explicitly announced.

## 3. Submission & Publication Model

- Rolling submissions
- 1–2 papers published per month
- No guaranteed publication
- Quality takes precedence over volume
- Papers are published only after successfully clearing all stages of review.

## 4. Paper Requirements

### 4.1 Word Limit
- 1000–1500 words (excluding references)

### 4.2 Mandatory Structure

All submissions must include the following sections:
- Title
- Abstract (100–150 words)
- Context / Background
- Core Argument
- Counterargument Section
- Policy or Strategic Implications
- Conclusion
- References

**Submissions that do not follow this structure will not proceed to peer review.**

## 5. Acceptable Topics

Submissions may address:
- International relations
- Public policy
- Institutional governance
- Strategic affairs
- Regulatory developments
- Media ethics
- Crisis decision-making
- Democratic processes

### Explicitly Disallowed
- Partisan propaganda
- Personal attacks
- Unverified allegations
- Defamatory content
- Hate speech
- Campaign-style persuasion

These restrictions exist to protect members and the society institutionally and legally.

## 6. Submission Guidelines

- Submit via the official Diplomacia submission form
- File format: PDF only
- Remove your name from the manuscript for blind review
- AI usage must be disclosed (if used for grammar/editing assistance)
- Authors must certify that:
  - The work is original
  - No plagiarism is present
  - All claims are properly cited

Non-compliance results in automatic rejection.

## 7. Review Process

### Stage 1: Initial Screening (Core Team)

Checks include:
- Structural compliance
- Plagiarism screening
- Tone moderation
- Basic formatting verification

Non-compliant submissions will be returned.

### Stage 2: Blind Peer Review

Each paper is evaluated by two independent reviewers.

**Review criteria:**
- Clarity of Argument (20%)
- Evidence and Sources (20%)
- Logical Coherence (20%)
- Engagement with Counterarguments (15%)
- Analytical Depth (15%)
- Writing Quality (10%)

Reviewers submit:
- Numerical score
- Structured written feedback
- Recommendation: Accept / Revise / Reject

### Stage 3: Revision (If Required)

- Authors have 7 days to resubmit
- Only one revision cycle permitted
- Revised drafts are re-evaluated by the same reviewers

### Stage 4: Final Vetting

The Core Team conducts final evaluation based on:
- Alignment with Diplomacia standards
- Tone neutrality
- Institutional risk assessment

The Core Team's decision is final.

## 8. Publication Standards

Published papers will include:
- Author name
- Publication date
- "Peer Reviewed by Diplomacia" label
- Standard formatting
- Mandatory disclaimer

**Disclaimer:** Views expressed are those of the author and do not represent institutional positions of IITM BS.

## 9. Ethical Standards

Authors must:
- Avoid defamatory statements
- Cite all claims accurately
- Avoid plagiarism
- Avoid doxxing or personal targeting
- Avoid explicit political campaigning

Violations may result in:
- Disqualification
- Temporary submission ban
- Permanent removal from eligibility

## 10. Reviewer Code of Conduct

Reviewers must:
- Maintain strict confidentiality
- Declare conflicts of interest
- Avoid reviewing close associates
- Provide constructive, objective feedback
- Not share drafts externally

## 11. Recognition & Incentives

- Best Paper of the Month
- Annual Distinguished Paper Award
- Reviewer Recognition Credits
- Publication Certificates

Excellence will be formally recognised.

## 12. Archive Structure

Each published paper will have:
- Title
- Author
- Abstract
- Web version
- Downloadable PDF
- Citation format
- Topical tags
- Related papers

Over time, this archive will function as Diplomacia's structured knowledge repository.
